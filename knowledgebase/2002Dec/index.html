To EJB, or not to EJB? Take your pick!

by Andy Matthys-Pearce, Savvis Communications

About the Presentation 


In a December, 2001 JavaWorld article, "To EJB, or not to EJB?" , Humphrey Sheil discussed how to decide whether to use EJB for a given enterprise application. Ed Roman, author of Mastering Enterprise JavaBeans , includes a section about this topic in his book, and the online discussion groups have had much lively debate over whether and when to use EJB. This is definitely an interesting discussion, and the focus it has received is not surprising given EJB's status as kingpin of the J2EE platform.

However, rather than delve into this debate over when to use EJB or not, this presentation explores a strategy, based on two J2EE Patterns, that provides the flexibility to easily switch an application among EJB, local class, and Web Service-based business service implementations - without modifying presentation layer or business logic code.* Write an enterprise application and deploy it using EJBs - or not - take your pick!

* There is one notable limitation here. To be able to run the same application with or without EJBs, the data access method must not use entity beans. Many would argue, though, that in most cases, the alternatives to entity beans are preferrable, anyway.
