Title: Mechanical Sympathy
Presented By: Jacob Hansson
About the Presentation:

Mechanical sympathy on the JVM - building very fast systems with Java

In an age where CPU clock speeds is finally leveling off, the problems we address as computer engineers grow ever larger and ever more complex. Building systems that push the boundaries of these problems require a deep understanding of the machines, virtual and physical, that execute our code.

This talk draws upon lessons learned building high performance databases on the JVM, as well as the excellent work the mechanical sympathy community has done in the past years.

You will learn several layers of the complex problem of high performance programming — elephants-in-the-room such as GC and thread co-ordination, instrumentation and measurement, architecture, and specific examples of addressing bottlenecks.
About the Presenter:

Jacob Hansson works for Neo Technology, the commercial backer of the open source Neo4j graph database, as the project lead for the Neo4j Server. He recently moved to Missouri and is setting up a small Neo4j office in Columbia.
